# Task 2 – Build Your Student Digital Portfolio

**Module:** 2
**Marks:** 20
**Role:** Student Digital Ambassador

---

## Overview

This task involved setting up a professional digital presence across key platforms used by
employers, internship coordinators, and the academic community.

---

## Platforms Chosen

### 1. GitHub
A version control and code-hosting platform essential for any technical professional.
I created a profile README repository with my name, branch, year, institution, and a
learning goal focused on cybersecurity and systems research. Over the next four years,
I plan to use GitHub to host security tools, document CTF writeups, and contribute to
open-source cybersecurity projects.

### 2. LinkedIn
A professional networking platform widely used by recruiters, academics, and industry leaders.
My profile is set up with my current role as Cyber Domain Lead at KAVACH - VIT Bhopal,
my Education at VIT with specialization in Cybersecurity & Digital Forensics (2025–2029),
and skills in OSINT, Cyber Threat Intelligence, Digital Forensics, and Linux.
Over the next four years, I plan to use LinkedIn to grow my professional network,
share research and achievements, and find opportunities in VAPT, Blue Teaming,
and Forensic Analysis.

### 3. Kaggle
A data science and machine learning competition platform useful for building
analytical skills that complement cybersecurity work — especially in threat detection
and anomaly analysis. I created an account to explore datasets related to network
traffic and malware. Over the next four years, I plan to complete Kaggle courses,
earn certifications, and apply data-driven thinking to cybersecurity challenges.

---

## Reflection

Setting up these platforms gave me a structured view of what a professional digital
identity looks like from day one of college. I already had an active LinkedIn profile
with my role at KAVACH and my education at VIT Bhopal, which made this task feel
directly connected to real professional goals rather than just an academic exercise.
The most interesting part was writing the GitHub profile README — distilling my
cybersecurity focus, Linux enthusiasm, and OSINT work into a few concise lines was
harder than expected. This task reinforced that a digital portfolio is not built
overnight; it grows with every project, certificate, and contribution made throughout
your academic journey.
