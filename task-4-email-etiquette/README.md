## Reflection

Poor digital communication can have serious real-world consequences.
A well-known example is when employees post informal or emotionally charged
messages about their workplace on public social media — even without naming
their employer directly — and face disciplinary action or job loss as a result.
In one hypothetical scenario, a student venting about a professor in a public
group chat could have that message shared widely, damaging both their academic
relationship and reputation. What could have been done differently is simple:
use a private, trusted channel for personal frustrations, and always pause
before posting to ask whether the message would be appropriate if seen by
a professor, recruiter, or institution. Digital communication leaves a
permanent trail — treating every message as potentially public is the
safest default.
