## Overview

This task involved researching a real-world cybercrime scenario and producing
an awareness resource including a detailed case study and a practical prevention
checklist tailored for college students in India.

---

## Part A – Case Study

**Topic: Ransomware — The WannaCry Attack (2017)**

The case study covers what ransomware is, how it executes step-by-step, who it
typically targets, and the real-world consequences of the WannaCry global attack
that affected 230,000+ systems across 150 countries.

Saved as: `task-5-cybercrime/casestudy.md`

---

## Part B – Prevention Checklist

A 10-point "Stay Safe Online" checklist covering general cybersecurity hygiene
and India-specific UPI/financial safety tips, along with official reporting
channels (cybercrime.gov.in and Helpline 1930).

Saved as: `task-5-cybercrime/prevention-checklist.md`

---

## Reflection

What surprised me most while researching this topic was that WannaCry — one of
the most destructive cyberattacks in history — could have been almost entirely
prevented. A patch for the vulnerability it exploited had already been released
by Microsoft two months before the attack. Yet hundreds of thousands of systems
remained unpatched, including critical healthcare infrastructure. As someone who
works in cybersecurity and leads KAVACH's Cyber Domain at VIT Bhopal, this was a
stark reminder that technical knowledge alone is not enough — awareness and
timely action matter equally. The one habit I will personally change as a result
is setting automatic security updates across all my devices and never postponing
a critical patch, no matter how inconvenient the timing.
